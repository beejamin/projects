Heritage Trail

It’s no surprise to anyone who has ever heard about the Packers has also heard about their history. The history and heritage of the Green Bay Packers is one of the differentiating factors compared to other NFL franchises. One of the more underrated Packers thing to do while in Green Bay is to take the heritage trail. The Packers Heritage Trail is a nice tour that takes you to numerous places where history happened. The best part of it all, it’s free!

What to expect:

This tour can take you a little bit of time to go through, depending on what you all decide you'd like to see. Part of the trail is a 3 mile walk, which takes you in a bunch of different parts of downtown Green Bay. Some of the other places may require you to drive around for them. If you’re fan of history like I am, you’ll enjoy this tour. You get to see historical sites like Curly’s birth place home, his grave site, and Lombardi’s family home.


If you're planning on doing the Heritage Trail, you can check out the Heritage Trail Map and scout the places you're wanting to check out. If you're in from out of town without the means to drive around the city, the Packers offer trolley tours as well. You are able to purchase these before you come on your trip, this way you know you have them prior to coming to Green Bay.


Another nice part about doing this trail, you’ll get to walk along the CityDeck which is a really nice addition to the downtown Green Bay area. It’s a beautiful area to be at during a nice day. Along the way, you can stop at Copper State Brewing or Titletown Brewing Company for a cold one or some cheese curds during this walk. It’s a no brainer!
